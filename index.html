    • Scope and Limits

    Tools and Technologies: Augmented Reality (AR): We use this technology to connect real life with the virtual world so that the user can still have awareness about the surrounding when looking at the interface of the helmet. Holographic Display: This display is similar to the modern car. There will be a mini projector to display the image onto the glass surface. Because this will be less noticeable than if there is a high contrast screen, the user can clearly keep their eyes on the road. Surround sound record and playback: The helmet will have a microphone outside of the helmet to capture the noise of the environment and mimic that through the built-in surround stereo headphone. This feature will always turn on and not be adjustable, we make this decision to force the driver to keep awareness of the traffic. Bluetooth: this is the way to connect the helmet with your phone to play the music and display the google maps from your own personal device. This way the helmet does not need to connect to wifi, all wifi related functions can be processed on the user’s phone and it’s data. Google Glass, Maps and Assistant: we will try to buy the license from google to use their technologies and apply that on our product. We use their technology to control the whole system, apply the AR function and use GPS. Because we use Google Maps, it will show the direction and crowded roads to help the driver. Emergency Alert: when there is an acidence, the helmet will use a microwave to check whether the user’s still have consciousness. It will automatically call the medical service if the user has passed out.If the user still has their awareness, the helmet will ask whether they need it or not and wait for the user to answer. Voice Control: To make the driver keep both of their hands on the handle bar, we have to find a hands-free method for the user to interact with the helmet. We found out that a voice control system is the best option. There will be a microphone attached to the mouth cover part of the helmet, and through that microphone the user can command the helmet as they want. Recyclable material: Nowadays, electronic waste disposal is a big issue in the world. Electronic devices keep being improved and released every year, so everyone has to throw away the old ones to get the new design. By using recyclable materials, in future if we release the newer version, we can still recycle the one helmet.

    Testing: We will have some physical tests for a normal helmet. Firstly, we will undertake the impact test, the reason behind this test is the safety for the drivers who wear it. We have to make sure our product is durable enough to protect the users. Next will be the water proof test, because there will be rainy days, the helmet should resist rain water to protect the electric components inside. For the user interaction test, we will look for 10 to 20 volunteers. We will give 3 helmets to 3 people from that group to use for 3 days each time. After 3 days, those 3 helmets will be given to the 3 others from the same group. Then, we will ask them to give a report during the end of their tests. Each time they give us a report, we will have a day to adjust and fix the bugs. This will take 3, 4 or more weeks to undertake the testing, this will be dependent on the bugs and difficulties that we can find during the testing. We will finish the test if 3 groups in the row are fully satisfied with the result.
